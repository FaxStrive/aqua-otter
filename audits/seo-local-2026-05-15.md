## 5. Local Schema Markup — 2 / 10

### Schema detected

None. Zero structured data markup was detected across the homepage, contact page, about page, FAQ page, or service pages. This is a complete absence, not a partial implementation.

### Industry-correct schema subtype

For a water treatment / water purification company, the correct LocalBusiness subtype is:

```
@type: "HomeAndConstructionBusiness" > "PlumbingService"
or
@type: "LocalBusiness" (with description specifying water treatment)
```

There is no specific schema.org type for "Water Treatment Service" so the closest accurate parent type is `HomeAndConstructionBusiness` with appropriate `@type` array. Using plain `LocalBusiness` is acceptable but less precise.

### Required properties (all missing)

- `@context`: https://schema.org
- `@type`: HomeAndConstructionBusiness (or appropriate subtype)
- `name`: Aqua Otter Water Systems
- `address` with `streetAddress`, `addressLocality`, `addressRegion`, `postalCode`, `addressCountry`
- `telephone`

### Recommended properties (all missing)

- `geo` with latitude/longitude to 5 decimal precision
- `openingHoursSpecification` (currently "Mon-Sat" stated in text only)
- `url`
- `email`
- `areaServed` (array of states/cities)
- `aggregateRating` with `ratingValue`, `reviewCount`
- `sameAs` links (BBB, Yelp, Facebook, YouTube)
- `priceRange`

### Multi-location schema requirement

Each of the 8 office locations needs its own `LocalBusiness` block with its own `@id`, address, telephone, and `geo` coordinates. These should be implemented as separate JSON-LD blocks on each location page (once those pages are created) and as a `branchOf` / `parentOrganization` relationship.

### FAQPage schema

The FAQ page has 16 questions across 4 categories with no FAQPage schema. Per current Google guidelines, FAQPage schema is restricted to government and healthcare sites as of August 2023. Do NOT implement FAQPage schema — it will not generate rich results and may trigger a manual review signal.

### Missing schema priority list

1. `LocalBusiness` (HQ) on homepage and contact page — highest priority
2. `AggregateRating` on homepage and reviews page — critical for star snippets in SERPs
3. `Service` schema on each `/systems/` page — important for AI Overview visibility
4. `LocalBusiness` blocks for each of the 7 non-HQ offices (once location pages are built)
5. `BreadcrumbList` on all internal pages
6. `Article` schema on blog posts and /learn pages

Each city page must include:

- City-specific testimonials (route existing reviews to their origin city)
- Local links (city utility, water authority)

At minimum 40% unique content per location page is required; 60%+ is recommended to avoid duplicate content penalties.

## Industry-Specific Findings: Home Services / Water Treatment

### WQA (Water Quality Association) membership
WQA-certified dealers carry a strong trust signal in the water treatment vertical. There is no WQA badge on the site. If Aqua Otter is WQA-certified (or can become certified), the WQA dealer directory listing is a high-authority, industry-specific citation. WQA.org is a DA 50+ domain.

### License numbers not displayed
In Indiana and most states served, water treatment installers are required to carry a contractor's license. Displaying the license number on the site (as text and in schema `hasCredential`) is a trust signal for both users and Google's E-E-A-T evaluation. It is also a local link target: state contractor license lookup databases link back to businesses.

### Well water differentiation opportunity
The site positions well water treatment as a specialty. Rural counties in Indiana, Michigan, Ohio, Kentucky, and Tennessee have high well-water populations with no other local specialist competing for "well water treatment [county name]" queries. This is an uncontested keyword space that state hub pages with well-water subsections could capture rapidly.

### Review geographic diversity gap
All visible reviews come from Indiana locations (Noblesville, Fishers). For Michigan, Ohio, Kentucky, and Tennessee offices to rank in their local packs, those GBP listings need their own review pools. A review generation campaign should be run separately per office — not centralized on the Indianapolis GBP.
