### Experience details
The brand has the *raw material* for elite Experience scoring — 25 years, 5,000 jobs, 12-point in-home tests, six states — but the site converts almost none of it into citable content. "First-hand" voice appears only in microcopy ("The system pays for itself. Usually by year two."). There are zero detailed walk-throughs of an actual install, no documented water-test panels with numbers, no "we tried X for a client in Carmel and here's what happened" stories. For AI engines, this leaves the site indistinguishable from a generic competitor.

### Expertise details
Copy is technically accurate and uses correct industry terms. But expertise without provenance is invisible to AI: the blog byline is "Aqua Otter Team," the About page names no humans, and there is no Person/Organization schema. WQA (Water Quality Association) certification is the standard credibility marker in this trade and is not mentioned. Plumbing-license numbers are not displayed. The "certified specialist" claim on `/how-it-works` lacks the certifying body.

### Authoritativeness details
Domain-level signals are middling. The biggest gap is external validation: blog posts contain **zero outbound links** to USGS, EPA, CDC, WQA, NSF, or Indiana state water-quality data, even though one post references USGS hardness statistics. AI engines treat outbound citations as a trust signal; their absence reads as a closed-loop content garden. The reviews are concentrated on Google + BBB only; no Trustpilot, no Angi, no HomeAdvisor, no Yelp links surfaced.

### Trustworthiness details
The strongest dimension by far. HTTPS with HSTS (max-age=63072000), full NAP across 8 offices, dated reviews back to 2023, BBB A+ Accreditation, lifetime warranty, named-and-located testimonials, fresh privacy policy. Trust gaps: privacy policy is short (~280 words) for a multi-state lead-collecting business, no GDPR/CCPA section visible, no terms-of-service word count audit performed, no editorial/corrections policy on blog, no clear ownership disclosure ("family-owned" with no family named).

## Top 10 Findings

1. **Zero JSON-LD structured data on the entire site.** Homepage, About, Contact, Service-Areas, and all blog posts have zero schema blocks. This is a critical AI-citation blocker — AI engines rely on LocalBusiness, Organization, Person, Article, and FAQPage schema to disambiguate entities and lift passages.
2. **No named human anywhere on the site.** "Family-owned" with no family named; "specialists who test water for a living" with no specialist named; all blog bylines are "Aqua Otter Team." Trust dimension is capped without this. *(Byline: real owner is TBD — pending Luke verification; do not invent.)*
3. **About page is only ~180 words** for a 25-year-old, 6-state, 5,000-customer operation. This is the single most under-leveraged page on the site.
4. **No industry certifications cited.** WQA membership, NSF cert numbers, state plumbing-license numbers, and certifying body for "certified specialists" are all absent.
5. **Footer links to `/water-library` and likely `/locations/*` return 404.** Broken internal promises hurt both UX and crawl-trust signals.
6. **Blog inventory is only 3 posts** and 2 of 3 are below the FaxStrive template's 2,200–2,500-word floor. Softener-vs-no-salt is 650 words on a $4,000 purchase decision.
7. **Zero outbound citations in blog content.** USGS, EPA, CDC, WQA, Indiana State Department of Health are referenced verbally but never linked. AI engines penalize closed-loop content.
8. **Em-dashes appear in blog body copy** — violates FaxStrive editorial standard (no em-dashes anywhere in titles, body, or metadata).
9. **No proprietary data published despite owning a 5,000-home test dataset.** Average Indiana hardness by ZIP, iron prevalence in Michigan well water, PFAS hits per county — all could be original, citable content. None exists.
10. **No social proof beyond Google + BBB.** No Yelp, Angi, HomeAdvisor, Trustpilot, NextDoor links. No sameAs Organization-schema bridge to LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram. Brand-mention surface area is thin for AI entity recognition.

Default byline remains "Aqua Otter Team" until then.*
3. **[HIGH] Build the topical-authority hub.** Restore `/water-library` (currently 404) as a pillar page covering: hardness, iron/sulfur/manganese, PFAS, chlorine/chloramine, well-water contaminants, softener types, RO, UV. Add 8–12 spoke articles per the FaxStrive blog-article-template (2,200–2,500 words, 15+ internal links, BLUF lead, FAQ, schema, 3+ CTAs). Prioritize the four bigger-volume topics first: PFAS Indiana, iron in Michigan wells, no-salt myths, softener cost.
4. **[HIGH] Add 8 city pages under `/service-areas/{city}/`** — Indianapolis, Fort Wayne, Grand Rapids, Livonia (Detroit Metro), Columbus, Lexington, Nashville, Raleigh.
